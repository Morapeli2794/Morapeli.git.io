Introduction

3D design software refers to digital tools used to create and visualize objects in three dimensions, enabling designers, engineers, and architects to build realistic models that can be viewed, rotated, and tested before production. These programs help users explore how different parts fit together, identify potential design flaws, and enhance overall quality. Popular examples include AutoCAD, SolidWorks, Fusion 360, SketchUp, Blender, and Tinkercad—with Tinkercad being especially favored by beginners and students for its simple, web-based interface. Learning to use 3D design software not only builds technical understanding but also fosters creativity and problem-solving skills, making it a valuable asset in both education and professional fields.

Process Documentation

This week’s task involved applying 3D design skills to complete a practical project. The process began with reviewing the project requirements and selecting the appropriate digital tools. Participants used software like SketchUp and Fusion 360 to model their designs. They followed a step-by-step approach: planning the design, creating initial sketches, building the 3D model, and refining details for accuracy. Time was managed by setting daily goals and tracking progress. Challenges included learning new features, ensuring precision, and solving technical issues. By the end of the week, participants successfully completed their models and improved their confidence in using digital design tools.
