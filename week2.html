week 2: Design Thinking

Date: 7th to 13th Oct

Quick Summary

  • Skills Learned:In Week 2 of the Feb Lab assignment, we developed key skills in design thinking by learning how to define problems clearly, generate creative ideas, and build quick prototypes. We practiced user-centered thinking, collaborated effectively in teams, and made decisions based on empathy and insight. These skills helped us turn abstract challenges into practical, testable solutions.
  • Digital Tools and Software Used:During Week 2, we used GIMP and CorelDRAW to support our design sprint activities. GIMP helped us with image editing and creating visual elements for our prototypes, while CorelDRAW was used for vector-based design and layout work. These tools enabled us to bring our ideas to life with clear, creative visuals that supported our problem-solving process.
  • Topics: In Week 2, we explored key topics in the design thinking process, including problem definition, ideation techniques, user-centered design, and rapid prototyping. We also learned how to use GIMP and CorelDRAW to visually communicate our ideas and create low-fidelity prototypes that reflect real user needs.
  • Key Concepts: Week 2 introduced key concepts of the design thinking process, including empathy, problem framing, ideation, prototyping, and iteration. We focused on understanding user needs, generating creative solutions, and building quick prototypes to test ideas. These concepts helped guide our transition from identifying a problem to developing a practical, user-centered solution.
  • Challenges Faced:During Week 2, we faced several challenges, including defining the problem clearly, which required deep understanding of user needs. Generating innovative ideas was also difficult at times, especially when working under time pressure. Some teams struggled with using GIMP and CorelDRAW effectively, as they were new to the tools. Additionally, collaborating and aligning on one prototype concept proved challenging, but it helped us improve our teamwork and communication skills.
 

Introduction

Introduction Week 2 of the Feb Lab assignment focused on applying design thinking to move from identifying a problem to creating a prototype. This phase emphasized creativity, collaboration, and user-centered design, helping us turn ideas into tangible solutions.

Process Documentation

In Week 2, we followed a structured design thinking sprint to move from identifying a problem to building a prototype. The process began with problem definition, where we refined our understanding of the challenge based on user needs. We then moved into the ideation phase, generating multiple creative solutions through brainstorming sessions. Once ideas were developed, we selected the most promising one and proceeded to the prototyping stage, using GIMP and CorelDRAW to create low-fidelity visual representations of our solution. Throughout the week, we emphasized user-centered design, ensuring our prototype addressed real needs. We also documented feedback and prepared for iteration in the next phase.

Reflection

Reflection This week was both challenging and rewarding. It pushed us to think deeply about user needs and to collaborate effectively under pressure. Learning to use GIMP and CorelDRAW for prototyping was a valuable experience, though it came with a learning curve. The sprint taught us the importance of iteration and feedback, and how quickly ideas can evolve when tested visually. Overall, it was a week of growth, creativity, and practical skill-building.
